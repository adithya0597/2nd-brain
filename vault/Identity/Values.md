# Core Values & Beliefs

This file captures core values, guiding principles, and fundamental beliefs. Used by the AI to understand priorities and provide aligned recommendations.

## Values

1. **Growth over comfort** — Seek discomfort as a signal of learning. Stagnation is the real risk.
2. **Build systems, not habits** — Habits break; systems adapt. Design environments that make the right thing easy.
3. **Deep work is sacred** — Protect uninterrupted blocks. Shallow tasks expand to fill any space you give them.
4. **Ship imperfect, iterate fast** — A shipped 80% beats a perfect 100% that never launches. Learn from users, not assumptions.
5. **Relationships are investments** — Invest time in people who challenge and inspire. Prune relationships that only drain.

## Guiding Principles

### Work & Career
- Default to building in public — share process, not just results
- Automate before delegating; delegate before doing manually
- Every project should teach something transferable

### Health & Fitness
- Movement every day, even if just a walk
- Sleep is non-negotiable — 7+ hours, no exceptions
- Meal prep on weekends to remove weekday decision fatigue

### Finance & Wealth
- Save/invest before spending — pay yourself first
- Track every dollar monthly — awareness beats budgeting
- Build income streams that compound (products > services > time)

### Relationships
- Weekly check-ins with close family
- Be the friend who reaches out first
- Listen more than you speak in every conversation

### Learning & Growth
- Read 30 minutes daily — mix fiction and non-fiction
- One new skill per quarter, practiced deliberately
- Teach what you learn — it deepens understanding

## Beliefs Under Examination

1. **"AI will replace most knowledge work within 5 years"** — Currently exploring both sides. Building AI tools to understand capabilities firsthand. Counter-evidence: many tasks require judgment and context that LLMs struggle with.

2. **"Solo founders can build billion-dollar companies with AI"** — Testing this belief through Second Brain and portfolio projects. Counter: distribution and relationships still matter more than pure product quality.

3. **"Journaling is the highest-leverage personal development habit"** — Building this entire system on this premise. Need to measure: does structured journaling actually improve decision quality over 6 months?
