# Grill Report: Knowledge Graph Connection Quality

**Target**: `data/brain_demo.db` -- 96 nodes (90 document, 6 icor_dimension), 583 edges across 4 types
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Griller team**: 7 independent adversarial agents (zero shared context). All 7 produced full reports.

## Executive Summary

The knowledge graph has three compounding structural problems that undermine all downstream features:

1. **Mind & Growth is a semantic black hole.** The ICOR reference texts use generic knowledge-work vocabulary ("reading," "learning," "notes") that matches everything a knowledge worker writes. Investment portfolios, fashion projects, and trading bots all classify as Mind & Growth. 29% of affinity edges, 4% each for Health and Relationships.

2. **Grill reports form a parasitic clique.** 17 auto-generated review documents produce 140 of 211 tag_shared edges (66%) and 50 of 204 semantic_similarity edges. The graph is primarily a graph about its own construction, not about the user's knowledge.

3. **The graph is over-connected to the point of uselessness.** BFS from any hub reaches 96% of nodes at depth 3. Average degree ~12. Community detection, bridge nodes, and structural gaps are meaningless on a near-complete graph. The hybrid search (vector + FTS5 + chunks) does all the actual retrieval work.

The two highest-leverage fixes are trivially simple: exclude bot-generated reports from edge building, and add frontmatter overrides to ICOR affinity so explicit tags trump embeddings.

## Per-Lens Critiques

### Devil's Advocate
Scored every decision 1-3/10 defensibility. The three most damaging findings:
- **Empty files as top-weight edges** (1/10): "This is the most clearly indefensible finding. Empty files should never produce the highest-confidence edges."
- **Wikilinks silently dropped for Demo concepts** (1/10): "The system's most explicit connection type is silently failing for an entire category of files." (Note: this is by design, but the Devil argues the exclusion should be reconsidered.)
- **Mind & Growth gravity** (2/10): "The 29% vs 4% split tells you nothing about whether the user is neglecting Health... it tells you that Mind & Growth is a semantic black hole that absorbs everything."

Core argument: "A sparser graph with 150-200 carefully weighted edges would be strictly more useful for every downstream task than the current 583-edge hairball."

### Feasibility Audit
Found the graph infrastructure is sound engineering built on bad data. Key findings:
- **Semantic similarity uses title search, not content** (3/10): `search_similar(row["title"], ...)` on line 674 of graph_ops.py encodes the title string, not the stored embedding. "These edges are actually title-similarity."
- **Wealth & Finance references are all about job hunting** (4/10): "Zero mentions of investing, trading, portfolio management, or financial analysis." A pure data fix.
- **Investment-Portfolio has explicit tags the system ignores** (4/10): "The metadata is right there in the frontmatter."
- **Demo exclusion is correct** (7/10): Only decision rated favorably. "If demo files contain real knowledge, move them to vault/Concepts/."

### Bias Detection
Identified 3 systemic bias patterns:
1. **Builder-as-Oracle**: Every threshold was set by intuition without calibration infrastructure. No precision/recall curves, no holdout evaluation.
2. **Self-Referential Amplification**: The knowledge base about the knowledge base dominates the knowledge base. Grill reports crowd out life-content in similarity rankings.
3. **Snapshot Bias in Reference Design**: `_DIMENSION_REFERENCES` encode the user's life at one point in time (job-searching). As life evolves (investing, fashion), classification becomes misaligned with no mechanism for reference evolution.

Key finding: "Mind & Growth references use universal knowledge-work vocabulary. The builder wrote references that match their own dominant content, creating a gravitational well." Objectivity: 2/10.

### Cost-Benefit Challenge
Found semantic similarity edges are the worst ROI (1/10) and community detection is second-worst (2/10):
- **Semantic similarity** (1/10): "Built on title similarity (not content), include empty-file noise, and consumed by exactly zero user-facing features except community detection."
- **Community detection** (2/10): "Community IDs are consumed by exactly 3 things, one of which is dead code. Bridge nodes overlap with BFS results. networkx is 10MB for one function."
- **ICOR affinity** (2/10): "The tags_json classifier already does 90% of what ICOR affinity does. The remaining 10% is actively misleading."
- **Demo exclusion** (9/10): "Trivial implementation, prevents meaningful harm."
- **Embedding model** (7/10): "The backbone of the search pipeline. Fix the prefixes and skip daily re-embed, and this jumps to 9/10."

### Alternative Paths
The single strongest unexplored path: **Abolish the pre-computed similarity graph entirely.** Keep only wikilinks (74 edges) and ICOR hierarchy as structural edges. Replace semantic_similarity, tag_shared, and ICOR affinity with on-demand vector retrieval at query time.

"Your hybrid search with RRF fusion already outperforms BFS traversal for context loading. The 75-second graph build is computing and storing 487 edges that mostly encode noise. Every retrieval question is better answered by a 50ms vector query with metadata filters than by traversing a graph that connects everything to everything at depth 2."

Other alternatives proposed:
- **GraphRAG-style community summaries** instead of raw BFS traversal
- **LLM-assisted ICOR classification** instead of embedding cosine similarity
- **IDF-weighted tag similarity** instead of raw co-occurrence counts
- **Per-edge-type community detection** instead of collapsed multiplex graph

### Risk Amplification

## Blind Spots Exposed

1. **Semantic similarity edges use title search, not content embeddings** (Feasibility only). Line 674 of graph_ops.py calls `search_similar(row["title"], ...)` instead of using the stored file embedding. This means the 204 semantic_similarity edges reflect name similarity, not content similarity. The entire edge type is built on a bug.

2. **Reference texts frozen to a single life moment** (Bias only). The `_DIMENSION_REFERENCES` encode a job-searching, gym-going lifestyle. As the user's life evolves, classification drifts with no feedback mechanism.

3. **Nomic-embed missing task prefixes** (Cost-Benefit only). The model requires `search_document:` / `search_query:` prefixes for optimal performance. Missing prefixes cause 5-15% retrieval accuracy loss across all embedding-dependent features.

4. **Community detection outputs are largely dead code** (Cost-Benefit only). No command preset sets a `community_id` filter. Bridge nodes from communities overlap with BFS results.

5. **Duplicate Investment-Portfolio nodes** (Risk Amplifier only). TWO nodes at Goals/ and Projects/ paths plus a typo node `Investment-Porfolio`. Title-based queries resolve non-deterministically. This pattern likely exists for other files in both Goals/ and Projects/.

6. **37.5% of document nodes are stubs** (Risk Amplifier only). 33 of 88 document nodes have 1-50 words. Short files become "universal neighbors" in embedding space. The 2026-03-27 node has 39 edges and only 55 words.

7. **Undifferentiated edge types is the root cause** (Risk Amplifier only). All 8 challenged decisions are symptoms of one structural flaw: 4 semantically distinct edge types collapsed into one graph and traversed uniformly. Fixing individual symptoms without fixing edge-type-aware traversal is "whack-a-mole."

8. **No vault reconciliation mechanism** (Risk Amplifier only). Nothing compares files on disk vs. files in vault_nodes to detect silent data loss from exclusion rules, encoding errors, or parsing failures.

## Alternative Approaches Worth Exploring

1. **Abolish pre-computed similarity graph**: Keep wikilinks only, use vector search on-demand (Alternative Paths)
2. **LLM-assisted ICOR classification**: Replace embedding cosine with Haiku-class LLM call + user confirmation loop (Alternative Paths)
3. **Frontmatter-first classification**: Use explicit `icor_elements` / `icor_tag` metadata before falling back to embeddings (Feasibility, User Impact)

## Final Verdict

**Would a staff engineer approve this?**

**APPROVE WITH REVISIONS**

The graph infrastructure (schema, indexing pipeline, BFS, community detection, hybrid search) is solid engineering. The problems are all data-quality issues, not architectural flaws. Five specific fixes, in priority order:

1. **Exclude bot-generated content from tag_shared/semantic_similarity edge building** (5 lines in graph_ops.py). Eliminates the grill report clique that produces 32% of all edges. This is the highest-leverage fix.

2. **Add frontmatter override to ICOR affinity** (10 lines in icor_affinity.py). If a file has explicit `icor_elements` or `icor_tag` in YAML, use that instead of embedding cosine. Fixes Investment-Portfolio, AI-automated-trader, and similar misclassifications.

3. **Add minimum word-count filter to embedding/indexing** (1 line). Skip files with `word_count < 20` from embedding and edge creation. Eliminates the empty web clip noise.

4. **Rewrite Wealth & Finance reference texts** (data change in classifier.py). Replace job-search-only references with investment/finance/budgeting vocabulary. Reduces Mind & Growth gravity well.

5. **Fix semantic similarity to use content embeddings, not titles** (1 line in graph_ops.py line 674). Use the stored file embedding instead of encoding the title string. The 204 semantic_similarity edges are currently meaningless.

What is strong enough to keep: the hybrid search pipeline (4-channel RRF), the wikilink graph, the vault indexer, the classification pipeline structure, the event-driven post-write hooks. These are well-designed and working correctly.

What should be reconsidered entirely: community detection's value at this vault size (90 docs), the daily full graph rebuild (incremental updates exist but are not the default), and whether ICOR affinity edges justify their maintenance cost vs. simple keyword classification.
